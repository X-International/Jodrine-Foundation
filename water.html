Providing Access to Safe Drinking Water

Access to clean water is a basic human right, yet it remains a significant challenge for many in the village of Mukusu, Gomba. At the Jodrine Foundation, we recognize the importance of clean water and sanitation in promoting health, dignity, and economic development. Here's how we're working to address this critical need:

At Jodrine Foundation, we believe that access to clean water is a basic human right. Our mission is to implement sustainable solutions that provide communities with reliable access to safe drinking water, promoting better health outcomes and improved quality of life.

Current Projects

Well Construction: We are currently in the process of building one well in Mukusu, which will provide a reliable source of clean water for the community. However, additional funding is needed to complete this project and construct more wells around the village. These wells are essential for reducing the burden of water scarcity and waterborne diseases on our community. By building wells closer to residential areas, we aim to minimize the distance community members must travel to fetch water. This not only saves time and energy but also enhances overall well-being and productivity.

Community Engagement: Alongside well construction, we are actively engaging with community members to promote hygiene education, water conservation practices, and the importance of clean water for health and sanitation.

Challenges we face

Limited Funding: Despite our efforts, funding remains a significant obstacle in completing the construction of wells. Additional resources are needed to finalize ongoing projects and expand access to clean water to more areas within the village.

Technical Limitations: Well construction requires expertise, equipment, and materials, which may not always be readily available. Technical challenges such as geological conditions and logistical issues can further delay progress.

Water Scarcity: Many communities face water scarcity issues, with limited access to clean water sources due to geographical constraints and environmental factors.

Health Risks: Contaminated water sources pose serious health risks, including waterborne diseases and infections, highlighting the urgent need for improved water infrastructure and hygiene practices.

Our Vision for the Future

Scaling Up Well Projects: We envision a future where every household in Mukusu, Gomba has access to clean and safe drinking water. With your support, we plan to scale up our well projects and build additional wells in underserved areas of the village.

Promoting Hygiene Education: Access to clean water must be accompanied by proper hygiene practices to maximize its impact on health. We are committed to promoting hygiene education and behavior change initiatives to ensure the sustainable use of water resources.

Join us in making a difference
